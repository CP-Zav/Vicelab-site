Why visual pharmacology?

The Altered State Archive exists because the gap between clinical pharmacological literature and operational festival contexts has real costs. People need to understand mechanism, not just lists of risks. A harm reduction note reads differently when you can see why it matters.

Each plate pairs a molecular mechanism diagram with a structured harm reduction profile. The diagrams are schematically accurate without requiring biochemistry training — interpretable by welfare workers, medical teams, and festival-goers alike.

Plates are canonically locked after verification. The archive expands as new substances enter the operational intelligence requirement, with priority given to substances appearing in festival drug checking and welfare incident data.
