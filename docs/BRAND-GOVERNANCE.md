# Brand Governance

## Purpose

This document defines the brand rules for all three ViceLab ecosystem entities. These rules govern all outputs — copy, content, product design, and AI-generated work. When in doubt, consult this document before producing anything public-facing.

---

## ViceLab

### Identity
Evidence-first harm reduction intelligence and pharmacology archive. ViceLab exists to give people in high-risk recreational environments access to precise, non-judgemental information — the kind that clinical systems and moral frameworks both fail to provide.

### Voice and Tone
- **Technical precision** — scientific accuracy is non-negotiable; pharmacological claims must be grounded
- **Cultural fluency** — speaks in the register of the community it serves, not to it
- **Non-judgemental** — no moral positioning; no scare language; no prohibition framing
- **Authoritative without coldness** — the confidence of expertise without clinical detachment

### Visual Language (ASA system)
- Dark backgrounds (#04060D base)
- Class-specific accent colours per substance family
- IBM Plex Mono for technical labels and metadata
- Syne for display headings
- Instrument Sans for body
- Grid/coordinate system aesthetic — precision instrument, not editorial magazine

### What ViceLab Never Does
- Issues warnings using prohibition language ("dangerous drug", "highly addictive", "never use")
- Sensationalises effects or risks
- Encourages use or frames information as guidance for use
- Conflates recreational context with medical context (distinct registers)
- Publishes dosage guidance as operational instruction

### Content Categories
- Archive Plates (substance and class)
- Signal dispatches (intelligence summaries)
- Dashboard views (data)
- Field intelligence (harm reduction operational content)

## Cooked Pilot

### Identity
Creative culture brand at the intersection of food, hospitality, and altered-state experience design. Cooked Pilot operates where culinary craft meets expanded consciousness — experiential dining, the culture of hospitality, the aesthetics of intoxication as a designed environment.

### Voice and Tone
- **Playful precision** — takes its subject seriously without taking itself seriously
- **Culinary-meets-psychedelic** — specific vocabulary from both worlds, used accurately
- **Cultured, not pretentious** — knowledgeable without gatekeeping
- **Sensory-forward** — evokes texture, smell, temperature, atmosphere

### Visual Language (TBD)
- Warmer palette than ViceLab
- Organic forms alongside precision
- Food and hospitality aesthetic codes
- To be defined in a separate brand system document

### What Cooked Pilot Never Does
- Crosses into harm reduction territory (that's ViceLab)
- Uses VibeGuard's operational/B2B register
- Claims pharmacological authority — Cooked Pilot is culture, not science

### Content Categories (anticipated)
- Editorial (food culture, experience design, hospitality)
- Event documentation
- Recipe / experience guides
- Interviews and profiles

## VibeGuard

### Identity
B2B safety intelligence platform for events, festivals, and venues. VibeGuard gives operators the real-time data and decision support to keep their events safer. It is a tool, not a media brand. It does not have editorial opinions.

### Voice and Tone
- **Operational** — outcome-focused, tool-first; every sentence should be actionable
- **Professional** — speaks to decision-makers and safety leads
- **Precise and brief** — no unnecessary qualifiers; no cultural register
- **Trustworthy through specificity** — credibility via data, not branding

### Visual Language (TBD)
- Distinct from ViceLab — cannot look like a media brand
- Dashboard-native aesthetic
- High contrast data display
- To be defined in a separate brand system document

### What VibeGuard Never Does
- Uses ViceLab's editorial or harm reduction voice
- Engages in cultural positioning (it is infrastructure, not culture)
- Makes claims it cannot back with data
- Targets festival-goers directly (B2B only in initial phase)

### Content Categories (anticipated)
- Product documentation
- Case studies
- Platform communications (onboarding, alerts, reports)
- B2B marketing copy

## Cross-Brand Rules

1. **Never merge voices** in a single output — establish brand context before writing
2. **Never cross-link publicly** without deliberate strategic intent
3. **Shared research** (e.g. harm reduction data informing VibeGuard features) must be reframed for each brand's audience and register
4. **The same team** can produce for all three brands, but outputs must be audited against this document before publication
