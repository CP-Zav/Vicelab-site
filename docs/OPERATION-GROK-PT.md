# Operation GROK-PT

**Type:** Social Growth + Audience-Building System
**Scope:** ViceLab · Cooked Pilot · VibeGuard
**Status:** Active
**Last updated:** 2026-03-18

---

## 1. Mission

**GROK-PT** = Get Real On Knowledge — Pre-launch Traction.

Build audience before product. Create pull, not push.

The goal is not to announce ViceLab — it is to make people feel like they've discovered something. By the time the full platform launches, the Wingmate Crew already exists, already trusts the voice, and already associates ViceLab with something real: science that doesn't talk down to you, harm reduction that doesn't sound like a warning label, culture intelligence that actually understands the room.

Three objectives. In order:

1. **Position ViceLab as culture + science** — the intersection that doesn't exist yet anywhere else
2. **Build an audience that trusts the voice** before they ever see a product or paywall
3. **Create a community identity** (Wingmate Crew) that spreads the network on its own terms

What this is not:
- Brand awareness for its own sake
- Content marketing that sells features
- Educational content with a health authority voice

Voice here: direct, fast, zero warm-up. First word matters.

## 3. Content Pillars

Five pillars. Fixed. Every post lives in one of them.

### Pillar 1 — Signal Drops
Short, punchy harm-reduction intelligence. One mechanism, one risk, one actionable frame. Not a list. Not a lecture. One thing.

> Example: "MDMA + alcohol doesn't just get you more drunk. Your liver is making a third compound — cocaethylene. It has greater cardiotoxicity than either substance alone. Different conversation."

Format: Single post or 2-frame carousel. Caption only. No infographic.

### Pillar 2 — Field Intel
Real scenarios. Situations people actually encounter at festivals, events, at night. The kind of thing someone wishes they'd known.

> Example: "Someone in your group goes very still. Not sleepy. Still. Eyes open but absent. Here's what that means and what to do in the next 60 seconds."

Format: Story-style. Short. Stakes are clear from line one.

### Pillar 3 — Lab Notes
Science made human. One pharmacological concept, explained without condescension.

> Example: "The reason MDMA feels like it 'doesn't work' the second time isn't tolerance in the usual sense. It's serotonin depletion — your brain's supply chain is empty and it takes weeks to restock."

Format: Medium length. One concept through. Never multiple mechanisms in one post.

### Pillar 4 — Wingmate Protocol
Peer safety behaviour. The social norm we're trying to install: watch your crew, know your mix, act before it's urgent.

> Example: "The wingmate check-in: one question, mid-session. Not 'are you okay?' — that gets a yes. Something specific. 'When did you last drink water?' 'You eaten anything today?' The answer tells you more."

Format: Protocol-style. Practical. Never preachy.

### Pillar 5 — Build in Public
Show the work. Control Tower architecture, archive system, product decisions, operational thinking. The behind-the-scenes that makes people feel like insiders.

> Example: "We just standardised the entire ASA plate system. 4 plates, one canonical template, governance docs, QA checklist. Why this matters for how we publish harm reduction at scale — thread."

Format: LinkedIn-weighted. Process-oriented. Lets the audience see the rigour.

## 4. Posting System

### Daily Cadence

| Slot | Content | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Anchor post | Full pillar content — one of the five pillars, fully developed | 1 per day |
| Supporting posts | Repost, reply, commentary, short-form variant of anchor | 1–2 per day |
| Engagement block | Active comments, replies to DMs, responding to relevant conversations | 1 session per day |

### Weekly Shape

| Day | Pillar Focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Build in Public — week framing, system updates |
| Tuesday | Lab Notes — science drop |
| Wednesday | Signal Drops — harm reduction intelligence |
| Thursday | Field Intel — scenario |
| Friday | Wingmate Protocol — peer safety norm |
| Weekend | Archive plate drops (Instagram), TikTok field content, reposts |

This is a guide, not a lock. Anchor pillar to the day; let the rest respond to what's happening.

### Engagement Block
One dedicated session per day. Not passive. Active:
- Reply to comments on your own posts before they go cold
- Find 3–5 posts in adjacent spaces (festival culture, harm reduction, event industry, nightlife) and add value — not promotional, genuinely useful
- Check DMs and respond

## 5. Hook System

The first line of every post is the only line that matters. If it doesn't interrupt the scroll, nothing else gets read.

### Rules

1. **First line must interrupt.** Not introduce. Not explain. Interrupt.
2. **Curiosity over explanation.** Open a gap before you close it. Never lead with the answer.
3. **No warm-up.** No "In today's post I want to share..." No preamble. No context-setting. Start in the middle.
4. **No corporate tone.** No "We're excited to announce." No "As we continue to..." No brand-speak.
5. **Specificity beats generality.** "MDMA" beats "stimulants". "72 hours" beats "a few days". A number, a name, or a place beats a category.

### Hook Formats That Work

| Format | Example |
|---|---|
| Counter-intuitive statement | "Drinking water at a festival can kill you." |
| Specific consequence | "Your liver is making a third substance right now." |
| Missed piece | "Nobody talks about the 72-hour window after MDMA. They should." |
| Scene-setting | "You're at hour 9 of a festival. Your friend just went very quiet." |
| Reframe | "Harm reduction isn't about not using. It's about not dying." |
| Direct challenge | "You think you know your mix. Here's the thing you don't know." |

### What Not to Do

- "Did you know...?" — overused, flags content as educational before anyone opts in
- "Important safety reminder..." — health authority voice, people scroll past this
- "We believe that..." — opinion framing kills authority
- Hashtag-first opening — signals low effort
- Emoji-first opening — signals low signal

## 6. Tone Rules

The voice that runs across everything.

| Correct | Not This |
|---|---|
| Peer to peer | Expert to layperson |
| Confident | Authoritative |
| Knows things | Announces credentials |
| Slightly rebellious | Edgy for its own sake |
| Takes safety seriously | Moralises about safety |
| Respects autonomy | Issues warnings |
| Evidence-first | Citation-dropping |
| Conversational precision | Clinical or academic |

**The core test:** Would someone read this at 2am at a festival and feel respected, not lectured?

If the answer is no, rewrite it.

Never: "Please note", "Important", "Always consult a medical professional", "We strongly advise", "Responsible use", "Stay safe."

Always: The intelligence delivered straight, with the implication that the reader can handle it.

## 7. CTA System

Soft CTAs only. No conversion pressure. The goal at this stage is trust and community formation.

### Standing CTAs

| CTA | Context |
|---|---|
| **Stay lit, not lost.** | General ViceLab sign-off. Harm reduction frame without being preachy. |
| **Know your mix.** | Posts about combinations, interactions, testing. |
| **Watch your crew.** | Wingmate Protocol posts, field intel. |
| **More coming.** | Build-in-public, tease of product or archive. |
| **The archive is open.** | Direct to ASA plates. |

### CTA Rules

- One CTA per post maximum. Usually none on short-form.
- CTAs appear at the end, after the value has been delivered.
- No "click the link in bio" construction unless genuinely necessary.
- Never lead with a CTA. Never frame the post as being about the CTA.
- The product does not get pitched. The community gets built. The product gets found.

## 8. Wingmate Crew

The community layer. Not a loyalty programme. Not a subscriber list. An identity.

### Definition

**Wingmate Crew** = people who look out for each other in the environments where ViceLab operates.

It is a cultural identity before it is an audience segment. Someone who sees themselves as a Wingmate Crew member:
- Knows their mix
- Watches their group
- Shares useful intelligence rather than hoarding it
- Doesn't moralize but does act when action is needed

### Behaviour We're Installing

| Behaviour | How We Reinforce It |
|---|---|
| Share field intelligence | Post formats that are inherently shareable to a friend who needs the info |
| Tag your crew | Wingmate Protocol posts that are explicitly written to be tagged at someone |
| Protect without policing | Language that normalises looking out without creating an intervention frame |
| Inform without judging | Consistent non-judgemental tone across everything we publish |

### Community Activation

- Acknowledge people who share Wingmate Protocol content
- Repost field intel from the community (with context)
- Ask for field intelligence — "what scenario do you want the archive to cover?" is an engagement mechanic and a content research pipeline simultaneously
- The archive plates are community artifacts: designed to be shared, saved, sent to someone who needs them

## 9. Content Production Flow

Every piece of content moves through this pipeline.

```
IDEA → HOOK → DRAFT → ASSET → REVIEW → PUBLISH → ENGAGE → LOG
```

| Stage | Action | Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Idea** | Identify source: signal from culture, archive topic, field scenario, build note | Raw idea, one sentence |
| **Hook** | Write the first line first. If the hook doesn't work, the post doesn't work. Iterate until it interrupts. | Hook line |
| **Draft** | Write the full post from the hook. Pillar determines format. Brand rules determine voice. | Full copy |
| **Asset** | If visual required: ASA plate, quote card, diagram, or photo. Reuse existing assets first. | Asset file or n/a |
| **Review** | Brand check: right voice? Right pillar? Non-judgemental? No corporate tone? Hook still works? | Approved or revised |
| **Publish** | Post at correct time for platform. LinkedIn: 8–10am weekdays. Instagram: 6–9pm. TikTok: 7–10pm. | Live |
| **Engage** | Respond to first comments within the hour. Active engagement signals matter to platforms. | Responses |
| **Log** | Record in ops/logs: date, pillar, platform, hook line, performance notes | Log entry |

### Speed Rules

- Ideas take 5 minutes to capture or they disappear. Use the log.
- Hook should be written before anything else. Not after.
- An approved post with no asset beats a delayed post with a perfect asset.
- Engagement is not optional. A post you don't engage on in hour one is a post you wasted.

## 10. Integration with Control Tower

All GROK-PT production assets live inside the Control Tower ops structure.

| Asset Type | Location | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Content prompts | `ops/prompts/social/` | Reusable prompt templates for generating each pillar type |
| Post logs | `ops/logs/social-log.md` | Chronological record of published posts, hooks, performance |
| Post templates | `ops/templates/social/` | Copy-paste structures for each pillar format, per platform |
| Idea bank | `ops/logs/idea-bank.md` | Unprocessed ideas and potential hooks, reviewed weekly |
| Campaign briefs | `ops/prompts/campaigns/` | Briefs for specific pushes (archive drops, product launches, events) |

### Naming Conventions

Post logs: `YYYY-MM-DD · [Platform] · [Pillar] · [Hook line]`
Templates: `tpl-[platform]-[pillar].md`
Campaign briefs: `brief-[campaign-name].md`

### Control Tower Rules for Content

- **Brand separation is absolute.** ViceLab, Cooked Pilot, and VibeGuard each have distinct voices and audiences. Content for one brand is never repurposed directly for another. A Signal Drop that works for ViceLab is not a VibeGuard post with the logo swapped.
- **Archive plates are ViceLab assets.** Sharing ASA plates on social is always a ViceLab action. VibeGuard may reference harm reduction capability; it does not share ASA content under its own brand.
- **Cooked Pilot content stays in its register.** Culinary-meets-psychedelic culture. Not harm reduction. Not B2B product. Different room entirely.
